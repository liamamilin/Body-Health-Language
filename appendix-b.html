Appendix B: Diagnostic Question Bank

This appendix provides diagnostic questions organized by stage of the body-health loop. Use these questions to inspect body state, energy, recovery, load, health decisions, and the relationship between body capacity and life demands. Each section corresponds to a stage in the loop.

Signal: Questions about What the Body Is Communicating

  1. What body signal am I noticing right now?
  2. Where in my body do I feel something?
  3. Is this a sensation, a discomfort, pain, or a symptom?
  4. What is the quality of this sensation (dull, sharp, throbbing, burning, tingling, tight, pulling)?
  5. What is the location of this signal?
  6. Does this signal radiate or stay in one place?
  7. Is this signal constant or intermittent?
  8. What changes in my body have I noticed in the past hour?
  9. What changes in my body have I noticed in the past day?
  10. What changes in my body have I noticed in the past week?
  11. Is my breathing shallow, normal, or deep?
  12. Is my heart rate fast, normal, or slow?
  13. Is my muscle tension elevated, normal, or low?
  14. Is my energy level high, moderate, low, or depleted?
  15. Is my appetite normal, increased, or reduced?
  16. Is my thirst level normal, elevated, or low?
  17. Am I experiencing any unusual sensations?
  18. What is my body doing that I have not yet paid attention to?
  19. If my body could say one thing right now, what would it be?
  20. Am I aware of any signals I have been ignoring or dismissing?

Awareness: Questions about Noticing and Observing

  1. When did I last check in with my body?
  2. How often do I notice my body state during a typical day?
  3. What prevents me from noticing my body signals?
  4. Do I tend to notice body signals immediately, or only when they become strong?
  5. Do I notice body signals more when I am still or when I am active?
  6. What helps me become more aware of my body state?
  7. What distracts me from body awareness?
  8. Can I describe my current body state in one sentence without interpretation?
  9. Can I notice a body signal without immediately reacting to it?
  10. Do I notice body signals early or late?
  11. What body signals do I typically notice first?
  12. What body signals do I tend to overlook or dismiss?
  13. How long can I maintain body awareness before my attention wanders?
  14. Does my body awareness change throughout the day?
  15. Is my body awareness higher on some days than others? What differs?

Interpretation: Questions about Making Sense of Signals

  1. What interpretation am I adding to this body signal?
  2. Is my interpretation based on evidence or fear?
  3. Am I catastrophizing this sensation?
  4. Am I dismissing this signal as unimportant?
  5. What is the most neutral description of this signal?
  6. What is the worst interpretation I could make of this signal?
  7. What is the most likely interpretation of this signal?
  8. What is a possible alternative interpretation?
  9. What information do I have that supports my current interpretation?
  10. What information do I lack that would help me interpret this signal more accurately?
  11. Have I experienced this signal before? What did it mean then?
  12. Is my body narrative about this signal accurate or distorted?
  13. Am I confusing sensation with meaning?
  14. Am I making this signal mean something about my overall health or about my worth?
  15. What would I tell a friend who described this same signal?
  16. Does my interpretation change when I am well-rested versus when I am tired?
  17. Have I checked my interpretation against objective data (sleep, food, stress, activity)?
  18. Is this interpretation helping me or harming me?
  19. What additional information would help me interpret this signal more accurately?
  20. Have I considered that this signal may have a simple, non-threatening explanation?

Pattern and Context: Questions about Recurrence and Conditions

  1. Is this signal a single event or part of a recurring pattern?
  2. When does this signal typically appear?
  3. What time of day does this signal occur?
  4. What day of the week does this signal occur?
  5. What activity was I doing when this signal appeared?
  6. What environment was I in when this signal appeared?
  7. How was my sleep before this signal appeared?
  8. How was my stress level before this signal appeared?
  9. What did I eat before this signal appeared?
  10. How much water had I drunk before this signal appeared?
  11. What was my emotional state before this signal appeared?
  12. Was I sitting, standing, moving, or lying down when this signal appeared?
  13. How long had I been working before this signal appeared?
  14. Had I taken any breaks before this signal appeared?
  15. Does this signal appear under specific conditions?
  16. Does this signal appear when I skip certain practices (meals, water, movement, rest)?
  17. Does this signal appear when I am under specific types of stress?
  18. What makes this signal better?
  19. What makes this signal worse?
  20. What have I tried that helped reduce or resolve this signal?
  21. What patterns do I notice when I look back over the past week?
  22. What patterns do I notice when I look back over the past month?
  23. Is there a correlation between this signal and any specific trigger?
  24. Is the pattern consistent or does it vary?
  25. What would I need to track to confirm or rule out a pattern?

Regulation: Questions about Nervous System State and Calming

  1. What is my current nervous system state: calm, alert, activated, or overactivated?
  2. Is my current arousal level appropriate for what I need to do?
  3. Do I need to up-regulate or down-regulate right now?
  4. Is my breathing fast, slow, or normal?
  5. Can I slow my breathing?
  6. Can I lengthen my exhale?
  7. Can I release tension in my shoulders, jaw, or hands?
  8. What would help me calm down right now?
  9. What would help me feel more grounded?
  10. Do I need to pause before continuing?
  11. Do I need to step away from the current stimulus?
  12. Do I need to change my environment to regulate?
  13. Do I need to move my body or rest my body to regulate?
  14. Am I trying to regulate through distraction rather than recovery?
  15. Is my regulation strategy actually restoring me or just escaping?
  16. Can I tolerate this body state without needing to change it immediately?
  17. Do I need regulation, not more pressure?
  18. Do I need regulation, not more information?
  19. What is my most reliable regulation practice?
  20. Have I practiced regulation recently, or have I been pushing through?
  21. Can I take three slow breaths right now?
  22. Can I feel my feet on the floor?

Recovery: Questions about Rest and Restoration

  1. When did I last truly rest (not just stop working)?
  2. When did I last feel fully restored?
  3. How was my sleep last night?
  4. How was my sleep quality this past week?
  5. How many hours of sleep did I get last night?
  6. Did I wake feeling restored or still tired?
  7. Did I wake during the night?
  8. How long did it take me to fall asleep?
  9. What is my sleep debt right now?
  10. What would help me sleep better tonight?
  11. Have I taken any real breaks today?
  12. What kind of rest does my body need right now: passive rest, active recovery, or deep sleep?
  13. Have I done any active recovery today (walking, stretching, gentle movement)?
  14. Have I had any screen-free time today?
  15. Have I spent any time outdoors today?
  16. Am I trying to replace rest with stimulation?
  17. Is my recovery deficit growing, stable, or shrinking?
  18. What would a fully restorative day look like for me?
  19. How much recovery time do I need per day to maintain function?
  20. How much recovery time do I need per week to maintain function?
  21. Am I giving myself permission to rest, or am I pushing through?

Load and Capacity: Questions about Demand and Available Resources

  1. What is my current load level: underloaded, stable, loaded, or overloaded?
  2. What is my current capacity level: high, moderate, low, or depleted?
  3. Is there a load-capacity mismatch right now?
  4. What is contributing most to my current load?
  5. Is my load primarily cognitive, emotional, physical, or metabolic?
  6. How long have I been operating at this load level?
  7. Can I reduce any source of load right now?
  8. Can I increase my capacity through recovery, nutrition, or regulation?
  9. How much of my capacity reserve is left?
  10. Am I operating from capacity or from reserve?
  11. How many hours of focused work can I do at my current capacity?
  12. How many hours of focused work have I already done today?
  13. What is the gap between what I want to do and what I have capacity for?
  14. Am I treating my capacity as if it is higher than it actually is?
  15. Am I underestimating my capacity and avoiding appropriate challenge?
  16. What would a realistic load for today look like?
  17. What is my fatigue level right now: none, mild, moderate, severe, or exhaustion?
  18. Is this fatigue normal or a sign of overload?
  19. Am I at risk of burnout?

Maintenance: Questions about Daily and Weekly Practices

  1. Have I moved my body today?
  2. Have I stretched today?
  3. How many hours have I spent sitting today?
  4. What was my posture like today?
  5. Have I changed position or stood up in the past hour?
  6. Have I eaten regular meals today?
  7. Have I eaten enough to fuel my activity?
  8. Have I eaten foods that support stable energy?
  9. Have I had enough water today?
  10. What is the color of my urine (an indicator of hydration)?
  11. Have I consumed caffeine after 2 p.m.?
  12. Have I had any alcohol today?
  13. Is my meal rhythm consistent or erratic?
  14. What is my daily routine for health: morning, midday, evening?
  15. Do I have a wind-down routine before bed?
  16. What health habits am I maintaining consistently?
  17. What health habits am I neglecting?
  18. What environment factors are supporting my health?
  19. What environment factors are undermining my health?
  20. What cues in my environment trigger healthy behaviors?
  21. What cues in my environment trigger unhealthy behaviors?
  22. What friction prevents me from doing health-supportive actions?
  23. What friction could I reduce to make healthy choices easier?
  24. What defaults in my environment lead me toward or away from health?
  25. Is my health system functioning, or am I relying on willpower?
  26. Have I done a weekly health review?

Health Boundary: Questions about When to Seek Professional Help

  1. How long has this signal been present?
  2. Is this signal improving, stable, or worsening?
  3. Is the intensity of this signal increasing?
  4. Is the frequency of this signal increasing?
  5. Is this signal interfering with my daily function (sleep, work, movement, social)?
  6. Have I tried self-care (rest, hydration, movement, nutrition adjustment) and seen no improvement?
  7. Am I experiencing any red flags (sudden severe pain, chest pain, shortness of breath, neurological changes, bleeding, high fever)?
  8. Is this signal unusual for me?
  9. Does this signal feel different from previous similar experiences?
  10. Is this signal accompanied by other concerning symptoms?
  11. Am I anxious or worried about this signal to a degree that is affecting my daily life?
  12. Have I been avoiding medical attention because of fear, cost, time, or denial?
  13. Would I advise a friend with this same signal to seek professional help?
  14. What is the worst that could happen if I delay seeking help?
  15. What is the worst that could happen if I seek help unnecessarily?
  16. What specific symptoms or criteria would trigger me to seek medical attention?
  17. Have I clearly defined my health boundary (persistence, worsening, functional impairment)?
  18. Am I using body-health language to delay care or to make a clearer decision?
  19. Do I need to see a doctor, a physical therapist, a mental health professional, or a dietitian?
  20. What information would I bring to a medical appointment to help the professional understand my situation?
  21. What is my plan if this signal does not resolve with self-care?

Learning, Building, and Transition Infrastructure: Questions about Body Capacity for Specific Life Domains

  1. Can my body support my current learning load?
  2. Can my body sustain the attention required for my study or skill-building goals?
  3. Is my sleep sufficient for memory consolidation of new material I am learning?
  4. Is my stress level low enough for effective learning?
  5. Is my energy stable enough for sustained reading, coding, writing, or problem-solving?
  6. Does my body have enough recovery capacity to handle the learning demands of this week?
  7. Can my body support the prolonged focus needed to build systems, write, or create?
  8. Is my emotional stability sufficient for the frustration and uncertainty of building?
  9. Is my capacity reserve adequate for the unexpected challenges that arise during building?
  10. Is my body supporting or limiting my ability to make sound judgments about my work?
  11. Can my body sustain the demands of my current career transition or life change?
  12. Is my stress tolerance adequate for the uncertainty of transition?
  13. Is my recovery capacity sufficient for the emotional ups and downs of transition?
  14. What is my energy runway for this transition period?
  15. How long can I sustain my current pace before I need a significant break?
  16. Am I protecting my transition infrastructure, or am I depleting it through neglect?
  17. What adjustment would increase my body’s ability to support my learning, building, or transition?
  18. If my body were fully supporting my goals, what would be different?
  19. What is the most important thing I can do for my body this week to support my long-term goals?
  20. Does my body feel like a resource for my goals or an obstacle to them?

Feedback: Questions about What Happened after Action

  1. What happened after I took action based on a body signal?
  2. Did the action I took improve the signal, worsen it, or leave it unchanged?
  3. What did I learn from the outcome of my action?
  4. Did I observe the feedback or did I move on without checking?
  5. What feedback did my body give me after I regulated?
  6. What feedback did my body give me after I rested?
  7. What feedback did my body give me after I ate or drank?
  8. What feedback did my body give me after I moved?
  9. What feedback did my body give me after I changed my environment?
  10. Did the outcome match my expectation? If not, what was different?
  11. What feedback from the past week is most relevant for my next adjustment?
  12. Am I paying attention to delayed feedback (e.g., how I feel the day after a hard workout)?
  13. What pattern of feedback am I seeing across multiple days?
  14. Is my current strategy producing the desired body-state outcomes?
  15. What feedback am I ignoring or discounting?
  16. What feedback have I received that contradicts my current interpretation?

Adjustment: Questions about What to Change

  1. Based on feedback, what should I adjust?
  2. Should I adjust my load, my recovery, my regulation, my nutrition, my movement, or my environment?
  3. What is the smallest useful adjustment I can make right now?
  4. What is the most impactful adjustment I can make this week?
  5. What adjustment would I make if I had to choose only one?
  6. Is there an adjustment I have been avoiding? Why?
  7. What adjustment would I recommend to a friend in my situation?
  8. How will I test whether this adjustment works?
  9. What specific outcome will tell me the adjustment is effective?
  10. How long should I try this adjustment before evaluating it?
  11. What might prevent me from making this adjustment?
  12. What would help me stick with this adjustment?
  13. Does this adjustment address the root cause or only the symptom?
  14. Does this adjustment align with my long-term capacity goals?
  15. What is my next adjustment after this one?
